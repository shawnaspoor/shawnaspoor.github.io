Buffet at Wynn Las Vegas

We tried eating here last time we were in Vegas but the wait was stupid long. Let's make it happen this time.

Description from the USA Today article on the best Buffets in Vegas:

"Steve Wynn's resort at the north end of the Strip, opened in 2005, is still a relative newcomer, but the property has already spent $99 million renovating its 2,700 rooms and suites, redoing the walls in neutral tones and introducing Warhol and Picasso prints as well as centralized control panels. If you love a luxurious gaming experience, Wynn's very popular poker room is the place to be. If you've cash to burn, try a $5,000 slot machine. The brightly floral, natural light-filled resort blooms with great dining venues; among them, Bartolotta Bistro di Mare, one of the country's best Italian restaurants (remarkably, most of Wynn's celeb chefs are regulars in their own kitchens). One of the Strip's best power lunches is at the resort's Country Club which overlooks the 18-hole, Tom Fazio golf course. The Wynn Tower Suites have a separate check-in area and their own pool. The massive Fairway Villa with its sweeping views of the course has its own massage room and wet bar."

Breakfast: $21.99+
Champagne Breakfast (Sat & Sun): $47.99+
Lunch: $25.99+
Dinner: $39.99+
