The National Atomic Testing Museum and the NTSHF

This museum looks pretty awesome, let's definitely make an effort to make it out to this place.

Here is how their website describes the museum:

"Imagine opening your history text book from grade school and having the ability to jump inside the pages and experience the events first-hand.

That's what it feels like when you step into the 10,000-square foot National Atomic Testing Museum. In late 1991, Congress chartered the museum as the country's only official atomic museum.An affiliate of the Smithsonian Institution, the museum houses interactive displays, short films, timelines and real equipment from the former testing site. You can even toy with some gizmos and gadgets, including testing your own radioactivity."

Price: $22

Hours:
Monday-Saturday from 10:00 a.m. - 5:00 p.m.
Sunday from 12:00 p.m. - 5:00 p.m.

Location:
755 E Flamingo Rd
Las Vegas, NV 89119

Mob Museum

I am a little worried that this museum might be light the Spy Museum in D.C., that was awful. But I am willing to give it a try.

Here is how their website describes the museum:

"The Mob Museum is a world-class destination in downtown Las Vegas dedicated to the thrilling story of organized crime and law enforcement. It presents an exciting and authentic view of the Mob’s impact on Las Vegas history and its unique imprint on the world. True stories of Mob history are brought to life in a bold and contemporary style via engaging exhibits, high-tech theater presentations and more than 600 artifacts, the largest collection of Mob and related law enforcement memorabilia under one roof. Since opening in 2012, The Mob Museum has accumulated numerous accolades, including being named one of “20 Places Every American Should See” by Fox News and Budget Travel magazine, “Las Vegas’ Best New Attractions for 2012” by Travel + Leisure magazine, “9 Reasons to Visit Las Vegas” by CNNgo, a finalist for the “Best Wider World Project Award,” by the British Guild of Travel Writers and “Best Museum” by Nevada Magazine and the Las Vegas Review-Journal."

Price: $19.95

Hours:
Sundays through Thursdays 10 a.m. - 7 p.m.
Fridays & Saturdays 10 a.m. 8 p.m.

Location:
300 Stewart Avenue
Las Vegas, Nevada 89101

The Neon Graveyard

I've wanted to see this place for years, it's definitely top of my priority list. Need to see it!

Here is how their website describes the museum:

"Founded in 1996, the Neon Museum is a non-profit 501 (c) 3 organization dedicated to collecting, preserving, studying and exhibiting iconic Las Vegas signs for educational, historic and cultural enrichment. The Neon Museum campus includes the outdoor exhibition space known as the Neon Boneyard, a visitors’ center housed inside the former La Concha Motel lobby and the Neon Boneyard North Gallery which houses additional rescued signs and is available for weddings, special events, photo shoots and educational programs.

Due to the nature of our collection, the Neon Museum Boneyard is only available to the public through an hour long guided tour. Tours are available seven days a week and tour times vary based on the season."

Price:
Day Tour: $18
Night Tour: $25

Hours:
Sunday-Thursday from 10:00 a.m. - 3:00 p.m.
Friday - Saturday from 10:00 a.m. - 7:00 p.m.

Location:
770 Las Vegas Boulevard North
Las Vegas, NV 89101
